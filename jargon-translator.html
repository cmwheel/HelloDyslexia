Jargon Translator

Every acronym you’ll hear from the school, in one plain-English sentence. Search or scroll.

Plain language · Link from every guide

504 (Section 504)

A federal law that gives students with disabilities the right to accommodations so they can access the same education as their peers; a 504 plan is the written document that lists those accommodations.

DIBELS

Dynamic Indicators of Basic Early Literacy Skills — a short, one-on-one reading assessment used to screen for reading risk and track progress; often used in the mCLASS system.

FAPE

Free Appropriate Public Education — the right of every student with a disability to receive an education that meets their needs at no cost to the family.

IEP

Individualized Education Program — a legal document that describes the special education services, goals, and accommodations for a student who qualifies under IDEA.

IDEA

Individuals with Disabilities Education Act — the federal law that guarantees special education and related services to eligible students with disabilities.

LRE

Least Restrictive Environment — the legal requirement that students with disabilities learn alongside their peers in general education as much as possible, with support.

mCLASS

A widely used reading assessment and data system that includes DIBELS; teachers give brief one-on-one subtests to screen and monitor reading skills.

MTSS

Multi-Tiered System of Supports — a framework schools use to give increasingly intensive help (Tier 1 for everyone, Tier 2 small group, Tier 3 more intensive) before or alongside special education.

NWF (Nonsense Word Fluency)

A DIBELS subtest that measures whether a child can sound out made-up words; it shows how well they can apply letter-sound skills.

OG (Orton–Gillingham)

A structured, multisensory approach to teaching reading and spelling that is often recommended for students with dyslexia; many programs are based on OG principles.

ORF (Oral Reading Fluency)

A DIBELS subtest that measures how many words a child reads correctly per minute from a passage; it reflects both accuracy and speed.

PSF (Phoneme Segmentation Fluency)

A DIBELS subtest that measures whether a child can break a word into its individual sounds (e.g., “cat” → /k/ /a/ /t/).

RTI

Response to Intervention — an older term for the idea of giving extra instruction and monitoring progress; often used interchangeably with MTSS.

SLD

Specific Learning Disability — a category under special education law; dyslexia is one type of SLD (often called SLD in reading).

SST

Student Study Team (or Student Success Team) — a school team that meets to discuss a student’s needs and plan interventions; it may recommend a formal evaluation.

Structured literacy

An approach to teaching reading that explicitly covers phonics, decoding, and language structure; evidence-based for students with dyslexia.

Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3

Levels of support in MTSS: Tier 1 is instruction for all students; Tier 2 is extra small-group support; Tier 3 is more intensive, often one-on-one or very small group.
