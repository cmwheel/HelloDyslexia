Key Facts

What educators need to know.

Dyslexia is the most common learning difference — and early, informed intervention makes a lasting impact.

3–5 students per classroom

With 1 in 5 children having dyslexia, every classroom has students who need support — whether identified or not.

California SB 114

California now requires universal dyslexia screening in grades K–2. Understanding the law helps you support students and communicate with families.

Strengths-based approach

Dyslexic students often excel in creative thinking, problem-solving, and spatial reasoning. A strengths-based lens transforms outcomes.
