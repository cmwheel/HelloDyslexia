School Pathways

MTSS, 504, and IEP — what they are, what your child gets, and how to ask for the right support. Schools as partners, not adversaries.

What it is

Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS) is the school’s framework for giving extra help before or alongside special education. Many children flagged on a reading screener start in Tier 2 (small-group instruction) or Tier 3 (more intensive support).

What your child gets

Targeted reading instruction, often in a small group, using evidence-based methods (e.g., structured literacy). Progress is monitored. This is the typical first step after a screening flag.

How to request it

Ask the teacher or principal: “My child was flagged on the reading screener. What tier of support are they in, and what instruction are they receiving?” Request the intervention plan and progress data in writing.

If the school says no or “wait and see”

Put your request in writing. Ask for the data that supports waiting. If your child is “well below benchmark” on two or more subtests, ask for a formal evaluation. Document dates and responses. See our Conversation Scripts for exact language.

What it is

A 504 plan is a written plan that gives accommodations so a student with a disability (including dyslexia) can access the general curriculum. It’s under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, not the IDEA special education law.

What your child gets

Reasonable accommodations: extended time on tests, audiobooks, text-to-speech, preferential seating, reduced copying, etc. No change in curriculum — just how they access it.

How to request it

Send a written request to the 504 coordinator (often the principal or a designated staff member). Request an evaluation to determine 504 eligibility. Provide any outside evaluations or screening results. Ask for a meeting to develop the plan.

If the school says no

Ask for the decision in writing and the reason. You have the right to an impartial hearing. Document everything. Consider an outside evaluation to support eligibility. Our Conversation Scripts include language for 504 requests.

What it is

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legal document for students who qualify for special education under IDEA. A child with dyslexia may qualify under “Specific Learning Disability” (SLD) in reading.

What your child gets

Individually designed instruction, accommodations, and sometimes related services. Goals are set and progress is reported. The IEP team (including you) meets at least once a year to review.

How to request it

Send a written request for an evaluation to the special education director or principal. Mention that you are concerned about a learning disability in reading (dyslexia). The school has a timeline to respond and complete the evaluation (varies by state).

If the school says no

You have procedural safeguards: request the denial in writing, ask for an independent educational evaluation (IEE) at public expense if you disagree with the school’s evaluation, and use mediation or due process if needed. Document all communication.

Remember

The goal isn’t just to “fix” reading — it’s to support reading while unlocking your child’s natural strengths. Good plans address both.
