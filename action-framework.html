30 / 60 / 90 Day Framework

A clear timeline so you know what to do when. Pick your child’s grade band, then follow the milestones.

Accountability · Linked to guides

Your child’s grade band

30

First 30 days

Understand the screener results using our Screener Guide. Schedule a meeting with the teacher. Document what you’re seeing at home (e.g., difficulty with letter sounds, rhyming). Use Signs by Age to see what’s typical for this age.

60

By 60 days

If the school hasn’t already, ask about MTSS placement and intervention. Request a formal evaluation if your child is well below benchmark on two or more subtests. Begin or reinforce structured literacy support. See School Pathways and Conversation Scripts.

90

By 90 days

Review progress data with the school. If response is insufficient, escalate (504 or IEP). Connect with outside resources if needed. By now, you should see your child as a whole person with a reading challenge and real cognitive strengths.

30

First 30 days

Understand the screener results with our Screener Guide. Schedule a teacher meeting. Document what you see at home (e.g., avoiding reading, frustration with decoding). Check Signs by Age for grades 3–5.

60

By 60 days

Request formal evaluation if appropriate. Understand MTSS tier and push for Tier 3 or evaluation if progress is slow. Ensure structured literacy is in place. Use School Pathways and Conversation Scripts.

90

By 90 days

Review progress and consider 504 or IEP if the school’s response isn’t enough. Look into outside tutoring if needed. Your child is more than their reading score — strengths matter as much as support.

30

First 30 days

Get the full screener report and interpret it with our Screener Guide. Meet with the teacher and/or interventionist. Document reading and writing behaviors at home. See Signs by Age for grades 6–12.

60

By 60 days

Request evaluation for 504 or IEP if not already in place. Ensure accommodations (extended time, audiobooks, etc.) and/or intervention are in place. Use School Pathways and Conversation Scripts for the right language.

90

By 90 days

Review progress and adjust the plan. Consider an independent evaluation if the school’s evaluation or plan doesn’t fit. By now, you should see your child as a whole person with a reading challenge AND a cognitive advantage.

90-day checkpoint

By now, you should see your child as a whole person with a reading challenge AND a cognitive advantage. Keep advocating for support while celebrating their strengths.
